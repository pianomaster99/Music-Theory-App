#Brilliant.org Brainlift
By Tom Liu
##Purpose of Brainlift
The purpose of this brainlift is to analyze how Brilliant.org works and pros/cons of Brilliant.org. The analysis will give insight into how I can create my own learning app.
##In Scope
- Rating the effectiveness of Brilliant.org's features
- Developing spiky opinions about Brilliant.org that require in-depth knowledge of the Brilliant app.
##Out of Scope
- Using AI to summarize reviews about Brilliant.org
##Analysis
###DOK1 - Facts
- Brilliant.org uses research from Harvard and MIT professors
- Brilliant.org teaches math and science
- Brilliant.org's lessons use interactive graphs and answer choices.
- Brilliant.org trackers user progress and recommends lessons based on experience
- Koji speaks all of his texts
- The UI uses lackluster colors and has lots of space
- One of Brilliant.org's periodic functions lessons allows wrong answers to pass check
###DOK2 - Knowledge Tree
- Brilliant.org's credibility is based on its collaboration with Harvard and MIT professors
- Brilliant.org's interactive features engages users
- Brilliant.org's progress feature helps students be consistent
###DOK3 - Insights
- Brilliant.org tries to maximize the user's duration of engagement with their website.
- Brilliant.org still needs to work on its lessons and UI
###DOK4 - Spiky Point of View
- While Brilliant.org aims to maximize the user's duration of engagement, it does not optimize the user's engagement, meaning that the amount and quality of content learned by the user is not optimal
- Brilliant.org's interactive features actually suck because I did not feel engaged at all, and the second lesson I did contained an error.
